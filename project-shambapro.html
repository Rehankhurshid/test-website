Project overview

Shambapro enables farm owners and farm managers to schedule farm activities, supervise farm workers, record and maintain secure farm records and make data-driven farm business decisions.

Here's the Landing page for the company of Shambapro to market their app which presents the benefits and how it can help the potential users. Brief was to plan content strategy & make it interactive and minimal. I did just that!

Landing Page Video

The problem

Shambapro needed a Landing page that shows the benefit of their product. The product is an App and it's not yet released. They wanted to make content that help user to understand the product and finally register for early access which will notify them when product releases.

steps

I focused on Content Strategy first. Content is king and that is one of the important thing in design. So I first went through the various effective and award-winning site to understand how they are handling this use case.

I have found that these are some of things that needs to be taken care to make it effective.

  • Screenshots/visuals —     demonstrates the functioning of the app
  • Punchy description — The elevator pitch that says why not clicking ‘signup’ is the     dumbest mistake they’ll ever make
  • Link to further content/info — This may be a pre-launch video, a blog, a press release, or any     other publicity your app has received.
  • Invite list call-to-action — The most important part! This gets     emails of interested people.
  • Refer a friend link – Gets more emails and creates a viral effect
  • Social profiles  — Links to follow your app on all your social media pages

Getting Inspiration

After considering and preparing the content for site along with the Founder, I started gathering inspiration from best sites that exist for this use case.

‍

Wireframing & Design Process

After going through various sites layout, we finally decided how the site will look. Then I started to making wireframes to get rough idea of how the website will be laid out. Then I discussed it with the Client about it.

I moved on to Figma for visual design of the site. I used the best practices to keep the project organised.

Finally after several changes from clients on design part, I went to Webflow for implementation of the site. Here the task was to take the site one step ahead by adding motion and animation.

Development in Webflow

I used best practices to design the site on Webflow and the client was quite happy with the result at the first glance. Then we did some changes in fonts and other elements to make it look good on every device.

The client also wanted to connect it with mailing services which can collect the mail when you register on site. So I decided to connect it via mailchimp. It will be helpful to further launch campaign and gain users.
